World's 3 Most Expensive Things

3rd Place- Bluefin tuna

Experts are worried about the future of tuna, officially a “threatened species.” But we’re still not sure if a single fish is worth a $3.1 million price tag. Kiyoshi Kimura, a sushi restaurant owner, paid that much for a bluefin tuna at Japan’s Toyosu market in early 2019, reports Reuters.

2nd Place- 1963 Ferrari 250 GTO

In 2018, the vintage Italian sports car broke a record when Chicago’s David MacNeil, the founder/CEO of WeatherTech, bought it for $70 million. As CNN explains, “It’s extremely rare for an owner to part with one at any price” because only 39 were built by Ferrari between 1962 and 1964. At the time of MacNeil’s purchase, the auction chairman James Knight said, “The Ferrari 250 GTO is the motoring market’s equivalent of Van Gogh’s ‘Sunflowers.'” Who else owns this legendary vehicle? Ralph Lauren, Pink Floyd drummer Nick Mason, and British billionaire Lord Anthony Bamford.


1st Place- Graff Diamonds Hallucination watch

Completed in 2014, the Hallucination watch by London-based Graff Diamonds took “several thousand hours of work” and 110 carats to create, reports Money Inc. “A sculptural masterpiece,” the platinum quartz watch is encrusted in yellow, pink, blue, gray, and orange diamonds in different cuts.
